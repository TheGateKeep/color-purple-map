All Start
Celie starts being at Pa's home, then later lives in the home of Mr after marriage. During both, she seems to suffer in some way, but as she grows she seems to understand her suffering more. Shug appears in person here first, and spends a good portion of the story here, mainly staying later on due to Celie. She chooses her travels based on what she cares for, but before often travel based on her own desires. She is one of the more determined characters in the book, starting off as one who does not let herself be controlled, and faces little opposition in that regard. Nettie starts the story running from Pa and Mr, leaving Celie behind. This contrast her later lengths she goes to help the Olinka. Her travels are done for helping people, firstly herself and then for the Olinka and then for Celie. Sofia, despite being one of the most resisting characters when it comes to control over her, first with Harpo and then with the mayor, almost always stays within here. She fights tooth and nail to stop Harpo controlling her, and briefly leaves to some unknown place, before returning to be arrested and essentially enslaved. While Shug simply does and is not stopped, Sofia fights against those who try to stop her, and she has suffered for it. Mary seems to start off in the story as a rebound that Harpo experiences, but later starts to grow to be her own. She seems to mainly act off of others. Firstly with following Harpo, then beginning to act on her own due to Celie's advice, then being free to sing with Shug and Celie and living with Grady for a bit. It isn't suprising that her travels often follow others such as Harpo, Grady, and Shug.
Celie, Shug, & Mary 2
These three seem to travel here as an act of freedom. Shug simply goes as she never really faced restriction, while Mary and Celie must resist their respective spouses to leave. This very well shows the idea of marriage as a sort of prison in the book, as after leaving, Celie describes herself as being finally happy. The very fact that Celie had to stab Mr in order to leave gives marriage it's extreme nature of restriction. To give some of the activities these three engage in here, firstly Mary ends up with Grady briefly, growing and selling drugs. Celie and Shug's role mainly focuses on their relationship with each other during this portion, and by the time the book switches focus, Celie has travelled back to Georgia.
Nettie 2
After Nettie runs away, she stays with a family, being parents Samuel and Corrine and children Adam and Olivia. They desire to go to Africa for missionary purposes, intending to help the people there. On their first travel, Nettie, Samuel, and Corrine go to Harlem and collect funds from many of the people there. The citizens seems to see the African people as kindred, and therefore bery willing to contribute to these efforts.
Nettie 3
On the way to Africa, Nettie & co. travel to England breifly. They also later travel back here in order to stop a rubber company from deforesting and demolitioning the village, but ultimately fail.
Nettie 4
Further along, Nettie & co. go to Senegal and then meet the predident of Liberia in Monrovia. They find that many issues in the US are present in Africa too.
Nettie 5
Nettie & co. go to the Olinka people in Africa to do their work. During this, Nettie faces speculation and prejudice due to her lack of marriage and unknown relation to Samuel and Corrine, many thinking Nettie is married to Samuel. They find out that a rubber company is building a road through the village, and while the Olinka think it's for them, they eventually learn that the company intends to tear down the village if they so desire. Corrine dies in Africa
Nettie 6
After failure to provide for the Olinka, Samuel and Nettie end up getting together, and later after failing to appeal to the missionary society, end up going to finally see Celie at the very end.
